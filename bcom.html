Bachelor of Commerce




About BCom:
The B Com is a three-year degree focusing on business, economics, marketing, accounting, taxation and finance. B Com is the basis for further research. B Com courses can be divided into B Com-General, B Com Honors and B Com LLB. A BCom degree can prepare you for a career as a company secretary, investment banker, chartered accountant or accountant. The degree is also available in distance learning and online and full-time BCom programs.

Why Bcom?
The growth of the fintech industry, mutual funds and insurance presents many opportunities for the financial industry. Students who complete a BCom degree have prospects in finance and business. After BCom, many students enter Sales, which is also a lucrative career. Sales departments across all industries have a lot of room for improvement with their specialized knowledge. Students interested in finance or business will also find more opportunities.

Benefits of BCom
One of the main benefits of a BCom degree is the ability to find a good job and start working after graduation. Students can pursue higher education such as MBA or PGDM. A BCom degree provides a good foundation for both careers. The BCom can also prove to be a stepping stone for students who want to succeed in business administration or business administration. Students can study further and choose MCom. The demand for applicants with a Bachelor of Business Administration has increased over time in both the public and private sectors.

BCom Eligibility Criteria:
Prospective students must have certain skills and meet eligibility criteria to enter the BCom program. Although each school has different selection criteria and admission procedures, the basic requirements for entry to the program are:
Candidates must complete 10+2. However, the required percentage varies from school to college. Candidates should have studied economics in Grades 11 and 12 in a variety of subjects such as accounting, business sciences, math/options and/or economics. Applicants must meet the University/School's minimum admission requirements. Note: Different colleges have different minimum cuts
