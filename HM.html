Hotel Management




What is Hotel Management?
Hotel management is an area of the hospitality industry that includes overseeing the operations of hotel facilities. As a hotel manager ,you manage the operations of a hotel, resort or similar business that provides services and other services to guests.

What do you need?
To be a successful hotel manager, you need to have many skills and social skills. You must also have prior experience in the business.
Many managers will begin internships, working in hotels of different sizes, reputations, and locations to gain more experience. It's also a great way to complete your degree.

Hotel management importance
The aim of hotel management is to increase the number of travelers and guests who come to your hotel throughout the year while providing a variety of services and facilities.
You can talk about how it will help your visitors with a business idea, and you can attract good leads with a new business idea

Skills Required to Become a Hotel Manager:
Students who dream of entering the hospitality industry after completing hotel management must possess a variety of skills to be successful in the field.

Observation Skills:
Students should be able to understand the customer's needs and preferences as expressed by the customer. Most customers may not tell them what they want and it may fit the hotel. However, the hotel owner should analyze and understand the need well and make sure that he is serving the customer. Small changes in customer behavior can affect owners.

Situational Awareness:
Tackling the hospitality industry can be overwhelming. Problems are everywhere and can sometimes catch up with hotel staff. Undesirable problems and undesirable situations can only be solved by being aware of the situation and seeing problems before they happen. Being aware of the situation will help prevent many problems that can affect customers. Hospitality management programs provide students with on-site training through internships that help develop such skills on the job.

Teamwork:
A hotel or resort can only be built as a team. Running a hotel, from kitchen to office, front end etc. has many teams, and all teams need to be able to work and collaborate. Each team member needs to know what tasks are assigned to each so that he or she can tell the team appropriate tasks without interfering with each other's business.

Multitasking Ability:
Every hotel employee should be able to manage when someone is unable to fulfill their duties due to unavoidable circumstances. The hotel manager needs to be able to multitask because problems occur together, not one after the other, and the manager must be able to solve many problems at once.

Cooking:
Cooking is the most important skill in the hospitality industry. Hospitality management programs focus most of their time on teaching culinary skills. Students are taught different types of cooking, including different dishes. In cookery, students gain knowledge based on theory and practice.

Communication:
One of the most important aspects of the hospitality industry is communication between managers and employees. A positive attitude and willingness to assist customers with the goal of providing the best service is welcomed. Customers should be greeted with a smile.

Eligibility:
The minimum score required for the Hospitality Management course is 10+2. Students can choose a certificate or even a master's degree. Certificate courses can last from six months to a year, while advanced courses can take three years
